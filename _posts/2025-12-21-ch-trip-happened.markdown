It was mid August when I came across a X post about direct China flights opening from India and the round trip prices to Hong-Kong dropped. The Canton Fair was to happen around that time and browsing through the event page, Phase 1 happening 15th to 20th October was of my interest.

Pulling in Mr. A, Visa plan, flights booking
Landing at HKIA, Octopus card, Breakfast, city view, HSR to Shenzhen <>
(12 Oct)Day 1: SZ check-in, amazing infra and lane discipline, spotting Mi SUV and BYD cabs, All EVS including garbage trucks, SZ market exploration start:
- Huangbei Electronics Building, SEG plaza, Periphery consumer product shopping complexes <>
- Overwhelmed day, notes taken and some purchases made, scammed by SD card seller <>

Day 2: Wutong Mountain Trek
- China has some of the most beautiful landscapes in the world, but several are interior and not yet popular for foreigners (neither did we have time). So the plan is made to hike to the highest point of SZ, Wutong mountain peak. Easy and straight forward to reach the hike start point, then starts communication gaps to navigate <>
- The hike was fully mapped out and most of the part with stairs (narrow and scary ones) <> Met a few locals on the way and discussed about electronics markets, supply chain, income and economy. They were surprised to see foreigners hike up to there <>

Day 3: Shenzhen Market Repeat
- Exploration continues, much more time spent in the market out of the distilled sections. Made spare part and component purchases, and a drone kit. 7-Eleven came to save when it comes to hydration and quick snacks (with ingredient labels thoroughly checked!!!) <>
- Sellers are mostly super friendly and they come to your level to understand the requirement and make a trade. One of them offered tea and took us to their upstairs office space, explained the catalogue and we shared each other contacts.
- Last day at Shenzhen, so did some night errands around the area, got fruits and portable water.

Day 4 and Day 5: HSR to Guangzhou
- Travel was easy. Passport identifies as train ticket for HSR. For Subway/Metro, the payment works through scanning a QR code at the gates through payment super-apps like Alipay or WeChat. Blazing fast speeds through the outskirts of Shenzhen <> and then we start seeing rural areas, small patches of farmlands, synonymous buildings pained in all same colour. Then sights a cluster of skyscrapers towards the west. That's Guangzhou city - which is the Canton Fair venue. Got into the hotel, did a short city walk and called it a day.

Day 6: First Canton Visit, massive hall, variety of products from all types of consumer devices, toys to tractors and superbikes <>
Day 7: Guangzhou market hunt, Buddhist temple visit <>
Day 8: Canton final day and head out to HKIA, passed time at the airport for next day morning flight.
